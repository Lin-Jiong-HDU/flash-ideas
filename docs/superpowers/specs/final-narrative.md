# Eco Evidence — 最终叙事定稿

> 团队对齐版 | 2026-04-04 12:05 CST

---

## ✅ 一句话（给所有人的版本）

**Eco Evidence 帮任何社区把日常的自然观察变成有法律效力的生态证据。**

不是科研工具，不是社交平台，不是环保组织对接系统。
是一个**证据生成器**——让普通人的观察在法律制度面前被认真对待。

---

## 🧠 完整叙事链（已闭合）

```
普通人观察自然（拍照+记录）
        ↓
AI 识别物种 + 自动聚合数据
        ↓
一键生成专业级生态报告
        ↓
  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │            报告的三层价值                          │
  │                                                   │
  │  第一层（当下）：                                   │
  │  教育价值 — 学生学会用数据描述自然                   │
  │  社区连接 — 一群人一起做一件有意义的事               │
  │  记忆保存 — 长辈的记忆和现在的数据并列               │
  │                                                   │
  │  第二层（储备）：                                   │
  │  证据银行 — 持续积累，形成生态基线                   │
  │  （即使现在不用，数据本身有长期价值）                 │
  │                                                   │
  │  第三层（行动）：                                   │
  │  当威胁出现时 —                                    │
  │  全球几乎所有国家的（EIA）环境影响评估法规           │
  │  都要求公众参与环节。                               │
  │  有数据支撑的意见 ≠ 情感呼吁                        │
  │  → 法律要求决策者回应有证据的异议                    │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**链条不依赖任何单一环节。**
- 如果没有开发威胁 → 第一层和第二层的价值独立存在
- 如果出现威胁 → 第三层价值激活，且有法律效力

---

## 🌍 为什么这是全球通用的

**环境影响评估（EIA）+ 公众参与**不是加拿大独有的制度。
它是 1992 年联合国里约地球峰会后全球推行的标准。

> 全球 100+ 个国家有法定 EIA 制度，其中绝大多数包含公众参与环节。
> 无论你在印度 🇮🇳、巴西 🇧🇷、肯尼亚 🇰🇪 还是中国 🇨🇳。

**但现实是：** 大多数公众参与是无效的——普通人提交的是情感诉求（"请不要砍树"），没有数据，被开发商一句"缺乏科学依据"就驳回了。

**Eco Evidence 改变的是这个：** 让普通人也能提交专业级证据。

## 🎬 4分钟视频脚本（终稿）

### 0:00 – 0:30 ｜ 情感开场

画面：老人和孩子在自然中的照片/插画，安静的音乐

旁白：
> "My grandmother remembers fireflies lighting up every summer evening.
> I've never seen one.
>
> Around the world, people are losing species they grew up with —
> not in headlines, but in their own backyards.
>
> The change is slow. The evidence disappears with each generation.
> Unless someone writes it down."

### 0:30 – 1:00 ｜ 问题陈述

画面：iNaturalist截图 → 开发项目公告 → 法院/市政厅

旁白：
> "Tools like iNaturalist help us identify species.
> But identification alone doesn't protect anything.
>
> When a developer proposes to build on a wetland,
> the community shows up with stories. The developer shows up with data.
> Stories lose. Data wins.
>
> In over 100 countries, environmental law requires public consultation
> before development. But most citizens submit emotional appeals
> that decision-makers can legally dismiss.
>
> What if communities had real evidence?"

### 1:00 – 1:10 ｜ 产品介绍

画面：Eco Evidence Logo + 一句话

旁白：
> "Eco Evidence turns everyday nature observations
> into professional ecological reports —
> so communities can speak with data."

### 1:10 – 2:30 ｜ Demo 演示

画面：屏幕录制（有旁白引导）

旁白：
> "Here's how it works."
>
> **Step 1：** "Take a photo. Our AI identifies the species, records the time and location."
> （演示：手机拍照 → 识别出 Red-winged Blackbird）
>
> **Step 2：** "Over weeks and months, observations accumulate at each location. The system tracks what appears — and what disappears."
> （演示：时间线视图，多条记录，有的物种在某周后消失）
>
> **Step 3：** "When you're ready, generate a report. One click."
> （演示：点击生成 → PDF 渲染 → 展示报告封面）
>
> "The report includes: a complete species inventory, diversity indices,
> change trends, and — crucially — a section for traditional ecological knowledge,
> where elders can share what this place used to look like."
> （展示报告内页：图表 + "Community Memory" 板块）

### 2:30 – 3:20 ｜ Four Lens 展开

画面：图标/图示辅助

旁白：
> "We designed Eco Evidence with One4Earth's four lenses at its core.
>
> **Equity & Justice:**
> This tool is free, open-source, and designed for communities
> that don't have the budget to hire environmental consultants.
> A class of students can produce a report
> that holds the same weight as a professional assessment.
>
> **Two-Eyed Seeing:**
> Every report includes a 'Community Memory' section —
> a space where scientific data and generational knowledge
> sit side by side, not in competition, but in conversation.
>
> **Practical:**
> No expensive equipment needed. A smartphone and curiosity.
> Start observing today, and the evidence bank grows with every photo.
>
> **Systems Thinking:**
> Eco Evidence doesn't stop at data collection.
> It connects observation to visualization,
> visualization to reporting,
> and reporting to the legal mechanisms
> that exist in every country to protect biodiversity."

### 3:20 – 4:00 ｜ 收尾升华

画面：社区/人群/自然，渐渐温暖的色调

旁白：
> "You might never need to use this report.
>
> Maybe the wetland near your school will stay forever.
> Maybe the fireflies will come back on their own.
>
> But if they don't —
> if one day someone draws a line through that meadow
> and calls it 'progress' —
>
> the evidence will already be there.
> Collected by a grandmother who remembered the salmon.
> By a student who photographed her first heron.
> By a community that chose to pay attention.
>
> Eco Evidence.
> Because the best time to start recording was twenty years ago.
> The second best time is today."

## ✅ 叙事自检

| 可能的评委质疑 | 回答 |
|---------------|------|
| "这跟 iNaturalist 有什么区别？" | iNaturalist 做识别，我们做报告。它是输入端，我们是输出端。甚至可以整合它的 API。 |
| "报告真的有法律效力吗？" | 不是法律文件本身，但 EIA 公众咨询阶段，有数据支撑的意见比纯情感呼吁在法律上更难被驳回。 |
| "谁会真的用这个？" | 学校自然课（教育场景）、社区想保护某个绿地的居民（倡导场景）、任何长期关注本地生态的个人（证据储备场景）。 |
| "你们做了原住民调研吗？" | 我们不代表原住民发声，但我们在报告中预留了'Community Memory'板块，邀请任何社区加入他们自己的传统知识。这是一种开放式设计，不是我们定义什么算知识。 |
